  Abstract: Information selection plays a crucial role in how individuals navigate online content.   Published: 2026-04-15
  Authors: Alex Cloud; Minh Le; James Chua; Jan Betley; Anna Sztyber-Betley; Sören Mindermann; Jacob Hilton; Samuel Marks; Owain Evans
  DOI: 10.1038/s41586-026-10319-8
  Link: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-026-10319-8
  Abstract: Abstract Large language models (LLMs) are increasingly used to generate data to train improved models 1–3 , but it remains unclear what properties are transmitted in this model distillation 4,5 . Here we show that distillation can lead to subliminal learning—the transmission of behavioural traits through semantically unrelated data. In our main experiments, a ‘teacher’ model with some trait T (such as disproportionately generating responses favouring owls or showing broad misaligned behaviour) generates datasets consisting solely of number sequences. Safety evaluations may therefore need to examine not just behaviour, but the origins of models and training data and the processes used to create them.
